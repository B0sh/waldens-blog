This week I read [System Design Interview](https://www.amazon.com/System-Design-Interview-insiders-Second/dp/B08CMF2CQF) (volume 1) by Alex Xu. 

I had just read [Designing Data-Intensive Applications](/designing-data-intensive-applications/), so this book came off as much more of a refresher of system design. I'm actually relieved that there's a lot of overlap in the topics.

This book is unashamedly for someone who wants to cram for **the interview** as their top priority. I won't be able to know, but I feel like I would've been quite lost without the depth of explanation I got by reading DDIA slowly and carefully. Normally, I wouldn't be interested in *study for the test* type stuff, but I was interested in a different perspective about system design. 

The best part about SDI is definitely all of the examples. They walk through the designs of several real large scale applications like YouTube and Facebook formatted as an interview. I think it's helpful to get used to the flow of an interview.

Also it really gets drilled into you about defining requirements. Each solution begins with a section where the mock candidate asks requirement defining questions. That also helps with coming up with high level estimates about storage, memory, or cost. Recently, I've been having conversations with someone about a business concept, and I found myself thinking back to this book quite explicitly and trying to ask a lot of questions. 

There's a [volume 2](https://www.amazon.com/System-Design-Interview-Insiders-Guide/dp/1736049119/) as well that follows the same format with more examples, but I think I'll pass unless I have an interview coming up where I know I'll need that material.
