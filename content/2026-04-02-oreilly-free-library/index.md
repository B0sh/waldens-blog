I've been doing a lot of my [studying](/book-review-ai-engineering) at my local library lately. I wanted to take a look at a sample of a book I was interested in, but I was shocked to keep scrolling and scrolling. They just give the book away for free online?

It turns out just opening the [O'Reilly website](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/ai-engineering/9781098166298/) on my local library's WiFi automatically gave me access to the online book library. I missed the login screen at first somehow. 

I'm curious how they are implementing this. My best guess is that they are basing it off of the request IP and checking if its from a library, because I feel like any design where the library side has to run a process somewhere to generate accounts for my login would be difficult to deal with. They knew the name of my library too.
